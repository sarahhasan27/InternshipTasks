Genres

Some of my favorite genres.

Classics

The classics book genre consists of timeless literary works that have stood the test of time, captivating readers across generations. With their enduring significance and profound storytelling, classic books continue to be cherished and studied for their insights into human nature and the complexities of life.

Science Fiction

Science fiction is a genre of speculative fiction that explores imaginative concepts, often based on scientific principles and technological advancements. It typically delves into futuristic settings, alternate realities, or advanced civilizations, blending scientific ideas with creative storytelling.

Horror

The horror book genre is a captivating literary category that delves into the realm of fear, suspense, and the supernatural. It aims to invoke a sense of terror, unease, and dread in readers by exploring themes of the unknown, paranormal, and psychological disturbances.

Details

A deeper dive in my stack of books.

  • Dune

    Frank Herbet

    Set in the distant future, it follows the story of Paul Atreides, the young heir to a noble family who becomes entangled in a complex power struggle on the desert planet Arrakis.

  • How the King of Elfhame Learned to Hate Stories

    Holly Black

    The book revealed a painful history of betrayal and loss, leading the King to associate stories with heartache and deception. As a result, he became deeply disillusioned with the notion of storytelling and closed himself off from the world of imagination and fantasy.

  • Little Women

    Louisa May Alcott

    Set in the 19th century, it follows the lives of the four March sisters: Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy, as they navigate the challenges of growing up in Civil War-era America. The book explores themes of family, love, sisterhood, and the pursuit of dreams, highlighting the individual personalities and ambitions of each sister.

  • The Shining

    Stephen King

    The story follows Jack Torrance, a struggling writer and recovering alcoholic, who becomes the winter caretaker of the haunted Overlook Hotel in the Colorado Rockies. Isolated with his family, they gradually succumb to the malevolent forces within the hotel, especially their young son Danny, who possesses psychic abilities called "the shining."

  • The Court of Mist and Fury

    Sarah J Maas

    The story continues with Feyre, who survived Under the Mountain in the first book. In this installment, she deals with the emotional aftermath of her experiences and forms a new alliance with the enigmatic High Lord of Night Court, Rhysand. As Feyre navigates the politics of the fae world and discovers her own strength, she faces challenges, and a deeper understanding of herself and her place in this fantastical world."

  • The Midnight Library

    Matt Haig

    It follows the life of Nora Seed, a woman who feels stuck and regrets many of her life decisions. On the verge of suicide, she finds herself in a magical library that exists between life and death, overseen by her former school librarian, Mrs. Elm. Each book in the library represents a different life she could have lived had she made different choices.
