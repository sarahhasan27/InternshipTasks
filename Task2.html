"The Midnight Library" is a novel written by Matt Haig, published in 2020. The story revolves around the main character, Nora Seed, who finds herself at a critical crossroads in her life and feels overwhelmed by regrets and the feeling of being stuck. Through her journey in the Midnight Library, Nora grapples with questions of self-discovery, purpose, and the nature of regret. The story explores themes of mental health, regrets, the pursuit of happiness, and the significance of the choices we make in our lives. "The Midnight Library" is a thought-provoking and emotionally resonant novel that delves into existential questions while offering a message of hope and self-acceptance. It invites readers to reflect on their own lives, the possibilities that lie within their choices, and the importance of finding contentment in the present.

"Dune" is a science fiction novel written by Frank Herbert, first published in 1965. Set in a distant future where interstellar travel and political intrigue dominate the universe, the story takes place on the desert planet of Arrakis, also known as Dune.

The novel follows the young protagonist, Paul Atreides, who becomes embroiled in a complex web of political machinations, environmental struggles, and personal transformation. The Atreides family is assigned the stewardship of Arrakis, a barren but resource-rich planet crucial for the production of the spice melange, a substance with immense value and power in the universe.

As Paul adapts to the harsh desert environment of Dune, he undergoes a personal and spiritual journey, tapping into hidden abilities and knowledge. He becomes the central figure in a prophetic destiny as the long-awaited messiah figure known as the Kwisatz Haderach.
